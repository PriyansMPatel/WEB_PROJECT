EVENTS

Events helps riders connect.

Road Race

Road races are competitive cycling events held on paved roads and can vary significantly in distance and terrain. Cyclists compete to complete the designated course in the shortest time. Courses can range from relatively flat to hilly or mountainous, challenging participants' endurance, climbing ability, and tactical skills. Road races often involve strategies such as drafting (riding closely behind another cyclist to reduce wind resistance) and attacks (surges in speed to break away from the pack).

Mountain Bike Cross-Country (XC):

Mountain bike cross-country races take place on off-road trails, often through rugged and varied terrain such as forests, hills, and mountains. Riders navigate natural obstacles like rocks, roots, and uneven terrain. The courses typically loop through challenging landscapes, requiring both technical skills and physical endurance. Mountain bike XC races can be conducted over a single lap or multiple laps, with competitors aiming to complete the course in the fastest time.

Track Cycling Pursuit:

Track cycling pursuit is a timed event held on an indoor velodrome track. The pursuit involves two cyclists starting from opposite sides of the track and racing against the clock. The goal is to complete a set distance (usually 4 kilometers for men and 3 kilometers for women) in the fastest time possible. Riders often use specialized aerodynamic bikes and employ strategic pacing techniques to optimize their speed and endurance throughout the race. The pursuit requires a blend of strength, stamina, and tactical awareness, as riders must manage their effort and maintain a high average speed to achieve the best time.
